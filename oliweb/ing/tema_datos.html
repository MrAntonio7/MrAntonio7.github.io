World oil market

Throughout the world, more than 3 million tons of olive oil are consumed per year, of which Spain produces almost half, which indisputably places it as the world's leading power of olive oil. However, palm oil it is still the most used with some 42 million tons consumed, followed by soybean oil (soybean) with a world consumption of more than 37 million tons. The rapeseed (which we now sell on labels like nabine oil) and sunflower, also have a significant share, with some 22 and 12 million tons consumed respectively. Let's say that, although for the countries that defend the blessed Mediterranean Diet, olive oil is the queen of fats, but represents only 3% of the total oils consumed in the world, and remains a great unknown to the vast majority of countries. But something is changing, and countries like China, the United States or Australia, already They have focused on olive oil, and they are starting to increase their consumption and production too, with intensive and super-intensive farming systems that could threaten the hegemony of traditional producers such as Spain, Italy or Greece.
