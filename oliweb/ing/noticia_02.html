Andalusia, concerned about the decline in the production of olive oil

For the current campaign some 884,900 tons are expected, which represents a decrease of 15.8% compared to the previous

The scarcity of rain and the high temperatures experienced in late summer and early autumn seriously threaten the production of olive oil in Andalusia. For the current campaign some 884,900 tons are expected, which represents a decrease of 15.8% over the previous year and 7.7% less compared to the average of the last five seasons, according to data from the Ministry of Agriculture, Fishing and Rural Development of the Board.

Although the Ministry maintains that "they are estimations" and the real production will depend on the climatological evolution, it is expected that Jaén, the main producer, is the most punished province. Specifically, the production here will be around 360,000 tons, which is 28.5% less than last season and 16.5% below the average.
